In its base form,
Diablo II
does not support 32-player groups, as the game engine is hard-coded for a maximum of 8 players per game. However, if you are conceptualizing a 32-player MMORPG-style scenario using Diablo II's existing classes and spells, you can distribute roles across the seven classes as follows: 
Tank Role
These classes use high health, defensive skills, and summons to soak up damage. 

    Barbarian: The ultimate meat-shield with Battle Orders for massive HP buffs and War Cry to stun surrounding enemies.
    Paladin: Uses Holy Shield for high block rates and Defiance aura to boost the entire group's defense.
    Druid (Werebear): A high-HP transformation that can use Shockwave to stun large groups of mobs indefinitely.
    Necromancer (Summoner): Acts as a "meat wall" tank by using an army of Skeletons and a Clay Golem to distract enemies. 

DPS Role (Damage Dealer) 
These classes focus on clearing screens of enemies through physical or elemental damage. 

    Sorceress: High-speed elemental damage using Blizzard, Lightning, or Frozen Orb.
    Amazon: Ranged physical and elemental DPS with Lightning Fury (AOE) or Multiple Shot.
    Assassin: Burst damage via Traps (Lightning/Death Sentry) or martial arts finishers.
    Paladin (Hammerdin): High magic damage using Blessed Hammer, which ignores most monster resistances. 

Healer Role
Diablo II lacks a dedicated "Priest" class, but specific skills can provide direct or indirect healing. 

    Paladin (Holy Bolt/Prayer): The most direct healer. Holy Bolt heals allies it passes through, and the Prayer aura provides constant passive health regeneration to the whole party.
    Druid (Oak Sage/Carrion Vine): Oak Sage doesn't heal but massively increases maximum health, preventing deaths. Carrion Vine consumes corpses to restore health to the Druid, which can indirectly help sustain the frontline. 

Support Role
Support in Diablo II is exceptionally strong due to auras and debuffs that scale with party size. 

    Paladin (Aura Bot): Can run Conviction (lowers enemy resists), Fanaticism (boosts physical speed/damage), or Meditation (massive mana regen).
    Necromancer (Curser): Uses Lower Resist to boost elemental DPS or Amplify Damage to double physical DPS. Decrepify is essential for slowing bosses and reducing their damage.
    Barbarian (Shouter): Provides Battle Command (+1 to all skills for everyone) and Shout (huge defense boost).

In a theoretical 32-player MMORPG setting, the classes across the
Diablo
franchise can be categorized into traditional roles based on their core abilities and skill trees from all expansions (including Hellfire, Lord of Destruction, Reaper of Souls, and Vessel of Hatred).
Diablo I
& Hellfire
Classes in the original game have fewer restrictions. Any class can learn most spells from books, but stats influence roles.

    Tank: Warrior (high health/blocking) and Barbarian (Hellfire exclusive, highest HP and physical resistance).
    DPS: Sorcerer (max magic for high-level spells like Fireball and Chain Lightning), Rogue (fastest bow attack speed), and Bard (Hellfire exclusive, dual-wielding melee).
    Healer/Support: Monk (Hellfire exclusive, uses Healing and Holy Bolt efficiently) and any character utilizing the Healing and Resurrect spells. 

Diablo II: Resurrected
Roles are defined by skill trees and party-wide buffs. 

    Tank: Barbarian (Shout/Battle Orders), Paladin (Holy Shield/Defiance), Druid (Werebear/Shockwave), and Necromancer (Skeletal army/Clay Golem).
    DPS: Sorceress (Elemental AOE), Amazon (Lightning Fury/Multi-shot), Assassin (Traps/Death Sentry), and Paladin (Blessed Hammer).
    Healer: Paladin (Holy Bolt for targeted healing; Prayer/Cleansing/Meditation for passive regen).
    Support: Paladin (Offensive Auras like Conviction/Fanaticism), Necromancer (Curses like Lower Resist/Amplify Damage), and Barbarian (Battle Command for +1 skills). 

Diablo III: Reaper of Souls
 
This entry introduced the "zDPS" (zero-damage support) meta, where classes focus on grouping and buffing.

    Tank: Barbarian (Ground Stun/Ignore Pain) and Crusader (Iron Skin/Consecration).
    DPS: Demon Hunter (Multishot), Wizard (Meteor/Archon), Necromancer (Skeletal Mage), and Witch Doctor (Firebats/Gargantuan).
    Healer: Monk (Breath of Heaven/Inner Sanctuary/Epiphany) is the premier healer in the franchise.
    Support: Barbarian (Wrenching Smash to group enemies), Witch Doctor (Big Bad Voodoo), and Necromancer (Frailty curse).

Diablo IV: Vessel of Hatred
D4 uses "Shouts," "Vulnerable" debuffs, and "Crowd Control" to define roles.

    Tank: Barbarian (Challenging Shout/Thorns builds), Druid (Werebear/Bulwark), and Spiritborn (Gorilla Guardian style).
    DPS: Sorcerer (Ball Lightning/Ice Shards), Rogue (Twisting Blades/Penetrating Shot), and Spiritborn (Eagle/Jaguar high-speed strikes).
    Healer: Druid (Blood Howl with aspects to heal allies) and Spiritborn (certain Centipede/Life Steal sharing mechanics).
    Support: Necromancer (Decrepify/Blight for grouping), Barbarian (War Cry for damage buffs), and Druid (Cyclone Armor for protection).
