Examples (1-100 Levels System with 1-150 effective character levels). The most powerful characters will always be player leveled / earned / progressed from scratch characters (zero to hero player characters) to avoid sell out developer values, that like shit roll down hill and create a toxic local reward feedback loop that ultimately destroys and the spiritual intellect of the game world and eventually leads to ruination of greatness; it is not enough to live one must strive to thrive! Unyielding, Dream Bold! Premium / earned characters are for convenience / quick start and simple jump in and play most everything accessibility. Items are mostly standardized / 'pick up and go' with some upgrade potential for earned prestige players. Almost like super mercenaries; enough to feel progress and extra earned power and force multiplication in the larger group picture. Yet to not undermine the long term investment of player earned / fully progressed player characters starting from scratch. A fine balance of a powerful quick start, easy / simple to play, able to be capable and able to master yet with a trade off of long term investment to still encourage fresh player character hunger for the full earned character experience. Although to put player earned zero to hero, real time invested characters first.

Also fundamentally the difference of 1 to 100-150 in level is mostly skill / outcome influence potential / opportunity. Any character can help in a fight even if they are just support and low combat effectiveness. The design is to be close to real life where more bodies is a viable strategy if organized and well executed. 'Peasant rush' / 'Zerg rush' / 'Out number' is a viable historical / real life tactic and it should be found as an experience option. Raids can include any level and more the merrier in general. Low level players margin for success against an end game veteran is slim to none but a well placed spear / 'man catcher' can upset the fight just like in history / real life; premium character / heroes are a threat in contest. Skill based game play, real rules for real fun, Player > Profit.

Trade-skills are seriously limited, the theme of the premium character is game / group participation. Making currency and playing the market is reserved for long term players. Premium characters can still trade items and help purchase and increase demand for traded items yet their theme is to be combat worthy and limited in focus, not a free full capability fully branched out long term zero to hero player character. There needs to be limitations that set a theme and tone. It is not fair to be able to flood the player ecosystem with off the shelf cultivators, extractors and industrialists. The more in-depth features can be seen, interacted with on a base level (trade and increase demand) yet to be able to fully be a part of the market and 'world of things' and creativity of crafting and deep world / empire building the player must sate / satisfy this hunger for deeper influence via a player earned character or else the whole struggle is cucked, castrated, undermined by off the shelf infinite premium crafters.
The theme of tall / focused / specialized yet narrow / inflexible / Locked.

Premium characters can be multi-boxed like regular earned 'zero to hero' player characters to add value in investment; multi-boxing is limited yet supported. this should replace 'bodyguards' with a limited player party. At maximum 3 characters can be multiboxed with game world legal support. A balance of realities and to open up the world in a legal way for skilled players and train aspiring players alike without irrelevanting casual players; everyone can meaningfully contribute and margins for success to the end game will be open. Although if poorly managed it will be narrow, yet still relevant and rewarding none the less. Mountains for skilled players to climb, valleys for casual players to fall into; yet the valley river guides the weary and the mountain tops inspire awe and reward for the hungry.

Premium characters are almost like 'twinks' out of the box or heroes in MOBAs (simple to pick up yet limited in scope yet still relevant and threatening yet able to master; specialized game jumpers). Starts base levels with Fine Quality armour and weapon(s) of the appropriate theme (that are not used to be made free and sold for 'free money if given out freely'; economy friendly). It is possible to earn prestige / respect / honour / reputation with their faction / home / 'base' and obtain better Exquisite, Masterwork, Enchanted and even Heroic items for the advanced and earned prestige bestowed after hard work by a players accomplishments with the character via the faction / close nit faction rewards. Almost like research and or modules and or set player advancements. Premium characters can drive the player economy and or help the player group content for more options to progress in terms of better quality items / player relevant progression. This means that premium characters can trade with players.

There should be group content available to experience at all tiers of premium characters. this includes starting at level 10 and this level of player power could be used to get items / farm instances or other areas of group content / generally help a low level 'zero to hero' earned player character. These premium characters are cheap if not rewards / collectables / one should be at least given to a new player for free to help them have a foothold in the game world.

The effective level of a level 90 premium character can be increased with items and player skill / game prestige yet the base character cannot level up like a 'zero to hero' character. There must be limitations and exclusives for earned 'zero to hero' characters to provide hunger for the player base to play and 'unlock' the full game with these earned 'zero to heroes'. Premium characters should be specialized in scope and not undermine fully earned 'zero to hero' player characters. Premium heroes are not a substitute for fully earned 'zero to hero' player characters; they are a force multiplier and are threatening to engage in group content yet are not replacements for fully earned 'zero to hero' player characters; nor should this value change and be undermined for local short term short sighted goals and or sell out profiteering. Players > Profit.
