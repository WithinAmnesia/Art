Primary Uses of an Apostrophe

    Contractions: It replaces missing letters or numbers when shortening words, such as turning "do not" into "don't".
    Possession: It demonstrates ownership when added to a noun, like "Sarah's book" or "the cats' toys".
    Rare Plurals: It is occasionally used to pluralize single lowercase letters to preserve visual clarity, such as "mind your p's and q's". 

Common Misconceptions

    Regular Plurals: You should never use it to make a normal noun plural. Writing "apple's" instead of "apples" is incorrect.
    Decades: Decades do not require an apostrophe before the "s". Write "1990s" rather than "1990's".
