Primary Uses

    Contractions: It replaces missing letters when joining words, such as turning "do not" into don't or "it is" into it's.
    Possession: It shows ownership, usually by adding an 's to a singular noun (e.g., Sarah's book).
    Plural Nouns: For plural words ending in "s", the apostrophe is placed after the "s" without adding another letter (e.g., the parents' car).
    Omitted Numbers: It can stand in for missing century numbers in dates, like shortening 1998 to '98. 

Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Plurals: Do not use an apostrophe to make a regular noun plural (e.g., write apples, not apple's).
    Its vs. It's: It's strictly means "it is" or "it has". Its without an apostrophe indicates possession.
